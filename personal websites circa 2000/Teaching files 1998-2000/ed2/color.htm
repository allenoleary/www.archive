HTML Color Picker

To get the color (note the American spelling!) you want you can either :

  • Use hexadecimal codes.
  • Use colornames.

Here's a chart of colornames, codes, and samples. Click on the sample text to see the background change to that color. Are you text and background colors clearly legible?!

This is the netscape `216' color safe chart. This mean that the colors below will be supported by pretty much any SVGA (read 1990+) monitor running at 256 colors or more. You are much better off using the hexidecimal codes in place of the color names in your code unless you can gaurantee that your audience will be using netscape or IE 3 plus.

You can copy and paste the color values from this chart into your pages, just don't forget to include the # symbol and the quotation marks. For instance: <body bgcolor="#7fffd4"> or <font color="#7fffd4">
