What is?

The term was coined in 1978 by Richard Saul Wurman who was, funnily enough, an architect. It concerns the art of taking lots of data and organising it into usable information to give knowledge to the user. The ultimate aim is to allow the user to draw insight or wisdom from the original data. This article is a distilled version of his book 'InformationA Anxiety 2' and my thoughts on it.

Why 'Architecture?'

Architecture is a highly developed form of communication practice. For many centuries architects (whether professional or hut builder) have created shelter, function and meaning through the envelopment of space with built structures. In doing so they have worked out many advanced ways of dealing with a user (or inhabitant), the tasks a user must do and the feelings engendered in an inhabitant. Assumptions

IA works from the assumption that the clearer the information is, the better off you are. This doesn't hold true for all occassions though. Many art-forms deliberately ignore, subvert or abuse this idea of clarity, and to good effect. The principles of information architecture hold when you want to lever a users understanding of something, whether it be a guide-book for a city, a software program or a website. In these situations coherence and comprehension are shaped around the aquisition of knowledge or the performing of a task.

 

Data/Information/Knowledge/Wisdom

Ths current 'Information Technology' revolution is a misnomer. We have a Data Revolution, but not often an Information Revolution. People assume that more data is more power, but in fact too much useless data is a pain. The attempts to get, for example, email clients to filter out unwanted data is a clear indication that we don;t want need or use all data we tend to recieve. Whether we consider data useful will depend on our own personal goals and motivation. If this data is presented as good information, it's useful will increse.

  Data Information Knowledge Wisdom
Description Data is raw numbers or facts, without a context. Anything from news to stock-prices, if not made coherent, is pure data. Information is orgainsation and presentation of data. The organisation and presentation of data introduces bias. The organisation of data happens first and is conceptual in nature. Presentation also creates meaning, but deals in the renedering of the structure (whether through text, image, sound) Knowledge is gained through the use we make of information. Knowledge is gained through experiencing information in a multitude of ways first-hand, and sharing that experience with others. Knowledge is often best presented in conversational or story forms (like classroom teaching). Knowledge is neccessarily more personal and casual than information. With wisdom we can recognise patterns in knowledge and think for ourselves about how they connect. Wisdom is often demonstrated by applying knowledge in new areas or in novel ways. Wisdom is aquired through repeated and deep contact with a field of knowledge. It's hard to fake.
