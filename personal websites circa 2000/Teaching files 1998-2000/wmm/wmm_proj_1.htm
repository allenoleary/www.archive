Writing for Multimedia - Assignment One

The Ten Frames to tell a story exercise (maybe revisited?)

Due week Four

Use ten frames to tell a traditional linear narrative.

You may have done this before, but doing it this time I really want you to think hard about what each of the ten frames is doing and it's position in narrative. You can also add dialogue, but no descriptive text. Dialogue should be for just the frame we're looking at, and is optional.

Make sure you've got one frame doing the following things:

  • Setting up the characters and situation
  • The Catalyst and characters response (first turning point)
  • Mid-Point scene (second major turning point)
  • The Crisis
  • The Climax
  • The resolution

The rest of the frames will be dealing with the development section of the piece, and each should outline an action point.

Presentation:

Drawings. No marks for grapichics skill.

Points to consider:

  • When does the point of view change and why?
  • What's at stake for the central character(s)?
  • Where's out 'in' our point of empathy or understanding?
  • What does the character learn? Whay do we learn?
