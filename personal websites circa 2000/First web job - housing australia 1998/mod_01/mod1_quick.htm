1. The Dominance of the Market in the Production and Allocating of Housing

In Australia, most aspects of housing provision are undertaken by the private sector. Governments do, however, have a role in providing resources for public housing, and in establishing a framework for private sector provision, for example, planning and building regulations, taxation, and other financial subsidies. Not all countries are as dependent on market provision as Australia and set aside a larger role for government.
