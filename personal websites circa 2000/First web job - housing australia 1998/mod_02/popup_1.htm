2. Home Ownership in Australia

1 Income Levels and Ownership

One way of addressing this question is to dismiss some popular explanations. Particularly in the 1960s and 1970s, one common view was that home ownership was a measure of Australia’s affluence and that, as other countries became richer, they would emulate this preference. As a number of observers have pointed out, however, after analysis of comparative data on tenure and levels of affluence, there is clearly no relationship. Table 2 ranks countries in terms of affluence and also shows their level of home ownership. Switzerland, which has the highest level of income per capita in the world, has a very low level of home ownership. Australia, which ranks nineteenth in terms of income, has the third highest level of ownership after Iceland (second in income level) and New Zealand (twentieth in income level). Anyone seeking a pattern in this data would be hard pressed to find a relationship between income and home ownership.
