Flying into any Australian city on a clear day an international visitor is inevitably impressed by the red roofs houses sitting in detached gardeners for as far as the eye can see. In Sydney the red roofed landscape might be cut by the bright blue of the harbour and it’s many bays inlets and rivers but in other cities it is a picture of almost unrelieved red and green. The sprawling cities of predominantly detached houses is probably the most visible image of what is characteristic, and occasionally unique, about Australian housing

Picture here

Behind this image is a complex housing system with many other feature which are different from that of other countries or, even if similar, take on different dimensions. This web site takes us through the major characteristics of the Australian housing system, its’ policies and its problems.
