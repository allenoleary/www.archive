Works

Here's a bag of stuff; flash projects, hypertext fiction, plays, radio plays and even an essay. Decided to lump it all together because I don't see much difference between doing a flash thing and writing a play - the creative process isn't wildly different, and a theatre is as much a technlogy as the internet...

For commercial/freelance stuff, see my resume.



Current Projects

Fond Love and Kisses [stageplay for Downstage]

This one is going on September 2002. It's a play set against the 'furlough draft' incident in NZ during WWII. It mainly concerns two brothers, Will and Henry, and Will's financee, Fiona. There's a lot of shit goes down when soldiers return home for a rest and then one of them decides not to go back... I've developed quite a liquid style for this one to avoid boring WWII play syndrome!




Alotronic System Hex [flash toy]

Just playing with the idea of replicating a 'desktop' in flash, and making it a little quirky and funny. Particulary interested in testing 'swapDepths' and having a laugh. Not finished, but you can see how it's going anyway.




Hot Wheels [feature film]

Everyone's got one film in them right? Well I hope to have a lot more than that, so this is one I'm as training. Can't say what it's about, but it's a little wicked...




The Casanova Complex [interactive database art]

This one is hard work: making an online database system that alows users to enter words, then have those words compete with previous entries for a place in the final sentence. Casanova takes over every now and then and 'seduces' the database with his smooth talk!




Past Projects

Ride on Time [radio play] broadcast RNZ July 2001

This is a play I actually wrote in 1995. It's a comedy about the oldest cycle-courier in Wellington and his quest to get some sperm across town on a hot day for a client. Produced and directed by Stephen Danby, cast included Sean Allen, Dra McKay, Kevin Blackburn, Carmel Maglone, Madelaine Mac Namarra, Tim Bartlett and a few thousand others.




Stageone [coldFusion site] May 2001

I built this as a personal project to help the NZ theatre scene get connected. It's a fully functioning coldFusion database driven site that works. Only problem is I haven't got around to launching it yet!




Where in the World is Brian? [flash project] Feb 2001

This one tracks the whereabout's of my intrepid Dad. He likes sailing and does delivery trips, too-ing and fro-ing around the world, preferably near the equator. Also doubles as an email repository.




Materials and Processes [flash educational interactive] Jan 2001

I did this one [of 6 similar] while at Swinburne Uni. It's the best flashing I've done yet on a technical level. Unforetunately the design sucks - and I didn't do it! But it has full drag'n'drop, dynammic feedback and the graphing tool is magic.




Deviations [stage play @ lamama] Dec 2000

'Elbow Productions' of Canberra got developemnt money and staged this one in Canberra at the Currong and at LaMama in Melbourne. I had a ball, as I spent a lot of time with the director [Iain Sinclair, a RADA grad] as well as the lovely cast [Lenore, Pip, Micheal and Lucy].

Here's some reviews to read. See below for trailer...




Deviations [flash trailer for play] Nov 1999

This is a flash site for the Melbourne Production of my play 'deviations'. Well worth a look, particularly for the interactive 'test your deviance' section.




a thread [html hyperfiction] September 2000

Ah, the ironies... Never having brought a stitch from Country Road in my life, I won the Country Road Cyberwriting competition with this short hyperfiction. Won a brand spanking new HP Pavillion as well as a new printer and scanner. And a $500 gift voucher. The work itself is three slices from a relationship.




Housing Australia Animation [flash splash page] May 2000

Made this for a very dull University course - tried to sex-up the front end. The project fell through after I made this, but I still like it.




The Casino Project [hyperfiction] 1998-2000

I won the Swinburne Postgraduate Coursework Award with this one. A love stpry set in the (then new) melbourne Casino. The imaging is fairly primitive (I had never even opened a paint program before this!) but the ideas and structure is still satisfying, particularly the 'shopping' section.




Self Intra Network [hyperessay] 1997

My first web work. This kinda grew from a class project into something fairly substantial. Again, the design is quite primitive, but the structure and the writing still hold up very well. It's about the formation of identity in the age of the network. 7




The Vampyre Dances [stage play] 1996

Premiered at Circa theatre, directed by Murray Lynch, designed by John Parker, live music by Janet Rodderick, and with a cast including Peter Hambleton, Clare Wladron, Sean Allen, Jacob Rajan, Nicola Murphy and Malcom Murray.

This play was also on the English curriculum for that year at Victoria University.

Production Photos




'Weightless' [radio play broadcast by Radio NZ] 1994

Directed by Carol Dee and with Jonathan Hendry and Bronwyn Bardley in the cast, 'Weightless' is about a couple of flatmates who try to write a film together. The film, about a fishing viallge on the Wairarapa coast, starts as a serious piece, but the conflicting ideas of the flatmates soon see it denegrate into a shoddy thriller.




Taking a Bath [short film Wellington and Auckland film festivals + 'Eat Carpet' SBS Aus] 1994

Written by Stephen Bain and myself. Murray Lynch got some money out of the QEII arts council to make this short film. Stephen and I took the main roles (since we wrote them for ourselves!) and Nancy Brunning took the role of Flic, the explosive girlfriend.




'The Supermarket of Love' [stage play devised by cast] 1993

Stephen Bain, Merophie Carr and myself wrote and produced this play at Bats and later took it for a ride to Hamilton (Merophie's hometown). It is about a heartless game show host who falls in love with the ghost of Vivien Leigh, and starts to lose the plot.
