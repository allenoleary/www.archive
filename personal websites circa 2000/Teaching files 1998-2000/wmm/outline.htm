Writing for Multimedia: Outline

Tutor: Allen O'Leary (alotronic@netspace.net.au)

Aims of Course:

  • To understand the general principles and history of multimedia
  • To analyse and desribe the form and content of Multimedia Productions
  • To conceive and develop to paper stage a Multimedia piece

Outcomes:

At the conclusion of the course students will be able to:

  • Identify and analyse various types of Multimedia
  • Analyse, create and develop both linear and non-linear story forms
  • Conceptualise and develop a script for a creative piece of Multimedia

Assessment:

  • 20% attendance and class participation (note: attendance of more than 80% is compulsory)
  • Two projects of 20% each and one of 40% (note: all projects due on time unless arrangements made with me or John, if 2 weeks late the maximum mark will be a pass)

Week by Week:

Week 1 14/7/2000

Introduction to the course, intro to MM, a brief history of Media and technological change.
Week 2 21/7/2000

Story: Linear film and print narrative

Week 3 28/7/2000 Story: Multi-path narrative
Week 4 4/8/2000 Ideas: brainstorming, writing, theories of creativity
Week 5 11/8/2000 Communication: Expressing ideas through storyboards, flowcharts, scripts etc
Week 6 18/8/2000 Chunking and Text on the screen
Week 7 26/8/2000 Worlds: What is a world and how do you create one?
Week 8 1/9/2000 Characters: Pencils and people, a character kit
Week 9 8/9/2000 Information design: Translating worlds, characters and ideas into screen and interface design
Week 10 15/9/2000 Wrap up and student presentation
