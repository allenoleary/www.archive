On Off

On Off is a choreographic solo piece, inspired by a text written in 1876 by Annie Bell Hobbs, the daughter of a lighthouse keeper. When published in a children magazine, her touching portrayal of life on the tiny and remote Boon Island, barely big enough for two small buildings and a lighthouse, caused an outcry that demanded families of such postings should be sent back to the mainland.

In affinity with New Zealand neurosis, Annie - the Child - views the world from the safety of her predictable environment, but where 'the other' holds limited possibilities.

Does growing up mean experiencing more than On and Off?

"I'm not afraid because I'm on my own. Is darkness really the same everywhere?"
