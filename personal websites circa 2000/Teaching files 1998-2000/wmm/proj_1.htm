Writing for Multimedia

 

Brief: Make a series of ten 'frames' that convey a narrative.

Aim: To generate the idea for a story, and tell that story by choosing and illustrating ten moments that best advance the narrative.

Delivery: The ten frames can be delivered in any format, from paper to a series of still images to a director movie. No text is to be included in the frames. Assessment will not be effected by delivery medium. Those presenting on computer must not add any transitions between frames, nor add any sound.

Assessment: We will be looking for the following things:

  • A clearly defined story with a beginning, middle and an end.
  • Appropriate selection of ten frames to tell that story
  • An awareness of the use of the frame in the role of conveying the story.

We will not be looking for:

  • Advanced presentation techniques or methods
  • Visual excellence (you can have it, but we wont mark it in fairness to others who may be less fortunate!).
