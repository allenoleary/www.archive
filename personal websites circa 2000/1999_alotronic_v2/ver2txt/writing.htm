2000

'Deviations' gets some development Money

After the frustrations of 1999, it's nice to get some development money for 'Deviations'. Elbow Theatre, an up and coming company form Canberra applied to Arts ACT for two weeks of developement money for 'Deviations'. The time and locality have yet to be arranged, but I hope that a workshop will lead to a production.

'Ride on Time' gets a recording

After years of mucking around (four to be exact!) 'Ride on Time' a radio play for Radio NZ, has finally been recorded. Stephan Danby took the play into the Studio with a great cast, and should be putting all the noises in soon.

'Burnt' called off

After much reading and soul searching, Evan and I decided that there wasn''t enough good material to warrant going to the effort of doing the production at LaMama, and have cancelled the project.

1999

Deviations a near miss at Playbox

After lots of good noise, Deviations got knocked back for the short season of up and coming writers at Playbox in 2000. However I did do a useful rewrite after some good advice from Tom Healey (Acting Associate Artistic Director). Also recieved some very positive feedback (but again, no production) form the Griffin Theatre in Sydney. It is still lurking somewhere in Jane Waddells plans for Circa Studio...

Burnt @ LaMama

Evan Watts and I had this idea that we might do an evenng of themed short plays at LaMama. The plays would be from 3 seconds to 3 minutes long and respond to the provocation 'Burnt'. We have convinced Liz Jones at LaMama that it's a good idea, so have to find the plays now. We advertised through the Various State Writers Centres, and the ANPC's magazine.

Within the space of three weeks 'Devaitions' got two play readings, one as part of the Adams Playreading Season (run by Playmarket NZ) and the other organised and directed by Evan Watts at LaMama.

Bill Manhire, the Vic Uni lecturer for the 'Creative Writing' Course at Vic Uni, included a poem of mine (from 1986...) in his anthology of best works from the Course. 1995

'The Vampyre Dances' in Oceania Playwrights Workshop

Directed by Lisa Warrington (who used to teach me at Otago Uni) and dramaturged by the excellent Simon O'Connor. This play caused lots of controversy at the conference on account of appropriation issues. While I wrote it as a satire on land rights (and very critical of the pakeha) it came in for a lot of attention from Maori actors in the cast. I made substantial changes to the script and was seen by some to be 'giving in' to racial pressure. I even heard someone say I was 'a victim of cultural imperialism'. Quite a stressful time!
