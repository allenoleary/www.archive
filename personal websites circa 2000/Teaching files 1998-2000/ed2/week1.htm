History of HTML and how it relates to HTML structure

HTML on the WWW is a mixture of three strong influences:

  • SGML, the <tag> system that has been used for over 30 years to mark up electronic documents for publication
  • The Internet itself, particularly the motivations for the intial development of the WWW by Berners-Lee
  • Hypertext systems, particularly those driven by the WIMP paradigm

     

Web Publishing, why bother?

A brief overview of pros and cons:

  • Pro: Accessibility of content across the world
  • Pro: Relatively low cost
  • Pro: Relatively easy to learn the basics
  • Pro: There's money for it!
  • Con: Exciting HTML can be difficult to implement across all browsers and platform
  • Con: You can NEVER gaurantee what your pages will look like on various machines
  • Con: Low resolution medium (72 dpi screen)
  • Con: Despite Dreamweaver, advanced HTML is difficult without a little programming knowledge
  • Con: Current connections speeds limit breadth of content
