The Exchange

The Exchange is the first major play by Paul Claudel and opposes four characters - four ideals without compromise - a prey of the satisfaction of their personal interests. The plays dilemna is an irresistable exchange: a wife and her love (Marthe) for the money that will offer the real freedom to her young husband (Louis).

The action happens in one day as if the sense of life was contained in the present time and that by letting this moment pass, the characters would surely pass by their own lives. The first english version of Paul Claudel's poetic drama is a highly visual and imaginative adaptation, combining the rich metaphoric text to the choreographic precision that defines the style of Under Lili's Balcony.

"A magical amalgam of music, dance and language, the production of The Exchange by Under Lili's Balcony is theatre both thrilling and profoundly challenging. The music, from Jeff Henderson on the sax and Steve Cournane on drums and percussion, accompanies the actors, moulding and supporting their words and movements. Steven Marriott Lloyd's set is stunning with its grainy walss and polished steel floor, while Andrew Foster's lighting is superb. Original in concept, daring in design, an immaculately performed, The Exchange is the most exciting production in Wellington this year."

The Dominion 4 July '97
