Dreamweaver/fireworks Exercise: Making a basic rollover

1. Make an "off" state of a button in Fireworks by drawing a button on one layer and laying some text over it on a nother layer. Make sure you deselect the "share across frames" option.

2. Make a new frame by selecting "duplicate frame" from the frame palette flyout.

3. Move to the second frame, change properties of the box and text to the "on"state.

4. Select Frame 1 and export the image, add a meaningful title (roll1_off.gif). Do the same for Frame 2 (roll1_over.gif). Make sure you export it to somewhere in your current site

You now have two images ready to insert into your page in Dreamweaver

5. In Dreamweaver, open the page you want to add the rollover to.

6. Select where you want to place the rollover image (inside a table say) and use the object palette to insert a rollover image.

7. Select the _off and _over versions of your files as appropriate.

8. Preview the page.

Part Two

Use the frameset you created earlier...

1. Make three or four rollover pairs in fireworks and export.

2. In the "side"window in the frameset, make a table to hold the images (probably an invisible border) and insert the rollovers into the table.

3. Link the rollovers to load content into the "main" pane.

4. Publish the new pages and images into the folder you made for the frameset exercise.

You have one week to complete this exercise.
