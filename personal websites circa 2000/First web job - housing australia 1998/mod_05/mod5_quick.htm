5. The Relatively High Rate of New Housing Demand for an Advanced Industrial Society

Most advanced industrial societies now have relatively subdued demand for new housing. In. These countries have lower rates of household formation because of historically low birth rates, an aging population, and low to very little migration. Australia still maintains a relatively high rate of household formation although the rate is contracting and may approximate the low rates of demand of other equivalent countries in a decade or so.
