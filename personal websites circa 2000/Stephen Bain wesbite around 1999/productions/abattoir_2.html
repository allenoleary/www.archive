Set in a fictitious museum, The Abattoir is the story of an art work, 'Wild horse', which has escaped from its frame and is rampaging through the gallery and beyond, threatening to pull down not only the building but Society itself. A string of wild characters are involved in the dilemma and thrown into a world where nightmarish paintings challenge their creators and cultural institutions are focussed on image and profit.

The play was commissioned for the STAB season. From a story written by France Hervé, it has been workshopped with a company of nine performers plus technicians. Designed specifically for touring regional Art spaces, the play aims to provoke discussion into the role of Art in the community - In conjunction with the play was an Installation gathering Artist's responses to a letter announcing the show's unrestrained horse premise. (Emails, letters, poems, audio tapes, painting and video were displayed in the foyer).
