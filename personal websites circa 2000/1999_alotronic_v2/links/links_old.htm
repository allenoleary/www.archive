Links

Just a few from each section... email me a suggestion if you want to.

Top Sites: just great looking or very clever sites
Html, asp, cfml: the top techs for building sites
Flash: good uses and resources
Art: for or by the web
Cybertexts: sites with a literary bent
Academic: scholars and resources
Architectural: sites of architects or groups
Theatre: specifically theatre sites with a cyberculture focus

 

Top Sites

McSpotlight - http://www.mcspotlight.org/
This is as close as I get to being a political radical... Extremely well-resourced and factual site about the horror that calls itself Mc Donalds.

Design is Kinky - http://www.designiskinky.net/
This is a design site with a large number of links, all to web designers homepages. And not just boring big-guys, but lots of quirky smaller players.

Vir2l - http://www.vir2l.com
Vir2l is a webdesign firm with a solid pedigree from MIT and their imaging is fantastic.

The Remedi Project - http://www.theremediproject.com
A leading spot to keep an eye on web based multimedia and changing and challenging flash interfaces

Html, asp, cfml

Webmonkey - http://www.webmonkey.com
Always top of the tops for a quick intro to any web topic. Excellent intro to cfml as well.

Webreview - http://www.webreview.com
Sometimes more in depth than webmonkey. Particularly good on style sheets.

ASP today - http://www.asptoday.com
This is the only ASP site I visit because it has good beginners essays and scripts to steal.

Webreference - http://www.webreference.com/
Another excellent web resource with many articles.

Flash

http://www.devdesign.com/flash/
A flash discussion group, you have to follow your nose, but there's some good tips and tricks in there.

http://www.flashcentral.com/
Tutorials and news.

http://www.flashcentral.com/tech
Same sitemakers as above, but with an emphasis on advanced flash development and programming.

Art

rhizome - http://www.rhizome.org
A good resource for keeping in touch with web art and writing from around the globe. Good email newsletter as well.

adaweb - http://www.adaweb.com
A large repository of works for the web, with features. Not as good as it used to be...

Web Gallery of Art - http://www.kfki.hu/~arthp/
While there are plenty of art resources on the Internet, this one is breathtakingly vast and comprehensive: just about anything by anyone from the Italian Renaissance and Baroque periods.

Cybertexts

Grammatron - http://www.grammatron.com
Mark Americas Seminal hypertext.

Critical Art Emsemble - http://www.critical-art.net/
Critical Art Emsemble make web works and perfromances that critique cyber culture

Academic

Gregory Ulmer -
Site for this Miama based academic of writing processes for 'electracy'. Includes plenty to keep you thinking about how we might write for and by the network.

Electronic Writing at Swinburne University -
http://www.swinburne.infoxchange.net.au/media/halm316/links.htm
I help tutor this course, and the website by Lisa Gye has very good links to academic sites relevant to electronic writing - there's no point in repeating them here...

Ted Nelsons Homepage - http://www.sfc.keio.ac.jp/~ted/
Ted Nelsons Homepage at his current post at Keio University, Japan.

Deleuze and Guatarri on the Web - http://www.uta.edu/english/apt/d&g/d&gweb.html
A great kick-off point to study their works and ideas.

MediaMoo - http://www.cc.gatech.edu/fac/Amy.Bruckman/MediaMOO/
MediaMoo is a media related MOO hosted by the MIT Media Lab.

The Postmodern Generator -
http://www.csse.monash.edu.au/community/postmodern.html
The Postmodern Generator is a nice spoof on academic prose.

Architectural

Bernard Tschumi - http://www.tschumi.com
Official website for this Architect. Not a great looking site, but good essays and pics of his work.

Coop Himmelblau - http://www.coop-himmelblau.at/
Good site for this excellent practice. Some great stage designs here too...

Archinect - http://www.archinect.com
Very good site to keep in touch with whats on the web for architecture. They include a lot of experimental work, some just for the web, and update regularly.

Parc De Villette - http://www.la-villette.com/
Here's the official website for my curretn favourite Architectural project, the collaboration between Eisenmann and Derrida.

Theatre

dv8 - http://www.dv8.co.uk/index.html
Site for this well known British company. useful for production history and that's about it.

dance technology zone - http://art.net/~dtz/
This is the best performance technology site on the web. Lots to get you going, great links, essays...

pantheatre - http://www.pantheatre.com/
Visually terrible, but some interesting essays on the methods of pan theatre, Enrique Pardaus unique take on performance and creation.

Robert Wilson - http://www.robertwilson.com/index.html
Home page of Robert Wilson, having news, projects and sketches.
