Final Assessment Piece,

'Writing For Multimedia' Date Due: Worth: 40%

Select one of the following themes (or choose your own in consultation with your tutor) as a focus for creating a prototype of an interactive multimedia narrative.

1) Theme Parks

2) Subcultures

3) Family Histories

4) Travels

This piece may be either fictional or non-fictional, documentary, artistic, experimental, training, or informative. It must be interactive and can be designed as either a web, or fixed media, presentation.

Students are expected to produce a written document that conveys a concise impression of the narrative, aesthetic and interactive designs features that would make up the work, while rendering, or realising, some key aspects of the projected work.

The document must consist of:

1) Title and a one-line synopsis

2) A one paragraph synopsis

3) Illustration of the Interactive Pathways that present the narrative to the user, with a clear articulation of the ways in which narrative and story elements are linked to each other

4) Interface Design, including a mock-up of a navigational screen that shows how users will engage the content of the work. When and where decisions are made by the user, how much control they have over the way the narrative is revealed. You can have more than one navigational screen and the navigation may be less anchored or menu-like than a navigational screen suggests.

5) Character sketches where applicable, or a brief background of the key people or key issues to be tackled by the project

6) Brief description of the major storylines

7) Description of the narrative world. Its rules, belief sytems, heirarchies, appearance, politics, spokespeople etc.

You might also consider the relationship between any narrators you might use and their narrative world. They could be newcomers, critics, journalists, prisoners, people with a mental illness etc. Think about the possibility of inventing a character or narrator to tell your story.

8) Rationale. What does your project aim to achieve? In what ways does multimedia provide particular challenges and/or particular advantages in the realisation of this narrative and narrative world?

9) Students are expected to render an aspect of their project that conveys some of the metaphorical, thematic and narrative content of the larger work. This can be linear or interactive.

Note: Students are expected to illustrate aspects of their project to convey some of the metaphorical, thematic and narrative content of the larger work. These illustrations can be digital or physical.

Assessment

I will be looking for the following things:

  • An imaginative engagement with the medium of interactive multimedia
  • Clear articulation of the structure of the work, and the users path through it
  • Well developed and nicely shaped story sequences within the piece
  • A clear vision of the world of the piece
  • Developed 'characters' with real impact on the work
  • An understanding of the importance of interface design
  • An ability to present the piece as an integrated whole
